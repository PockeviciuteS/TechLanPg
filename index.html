Introduction

"Godd day evryone my name is....

Introduce yourself to the audience and clarify who you are and your relevant expertise. by diong this it will help build an immediate relationship between you and the audience. This gives you the chance to briefly clarify your expertise and why is it worth listening to. This will help establish your ethos so the audience will trust you more and think you're credible.

Introduce your presentation title/the question you're exploring. Make it clear to the audience when they should ask questions - some speakers set aside specific sections for Q&A and others prefer the audience to ask questions when they come to mind. By clarifying this during your introduction you will avoid potential disruptions

Aims and Objectives

"The aims and objective are..."

Your aims are meant to be hit or achieved. ake sure that they are clear and consise with your resentation. You can also use your aims as a structure to what your conclusion is about.

our objectives should closly interlic with the main body of your presentation. The objectives is the topic which you will be discusing through out your presenations.

Main Body

"Lets beggin with the first topic..."

The main body of your talk needs to meet the promises you made in the introduction. Depending on the nature of your presentation, clearly segment the different topics you will be discussing, and then work your way through them one at a time.

Conclusion

"To conclude..."

The conclusion is frequently underdeveloped and lacks purpose which is a shame as it's the best place to reinforce your messages. be sure to summarise your main points and their implications. This clarifies the overall purpose of your talk and reinforces your reason for being there.

Conclude your talk by thanking the audience for their time and invite them to ask any questions they may have. This is the perfect time to have an Q&A and clarify any misunderstanding.
